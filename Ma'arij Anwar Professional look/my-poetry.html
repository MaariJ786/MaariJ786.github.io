    Here are some of my grand-dad's poetry and below are some of mine too

    Atone

    What’s it worth the lives you wreck
    The blood you shed the grief you inflict
    The tears you cause, the smiles you ruin
    The terror you spread the horror you work.
    Perhaps not worth the dagger you stain
    Not worth even the bullet you waste.
    You kill for joy, you kill for mirth
    You kill for merit in heaven
    Or you just kill for fun?
    You spend on poor to find solace
    You marry the woman
    Whose husband you kill
    You tender the child you turn orphan
    Is that how you atone for sin?

    Alone

    Why I look at every face they ask. I also wonder.
    Why, what do I look for.
    But in vain whatever I wish to see.
    I am an alien , strangers all around,
    Yet no curiosity, no interest.
    This is a scientific world, an economic world,
    Cold mathematics, no love, no passion.
    Who are these people, what country?
    Why no friends, no acquaintances?
    Who do I look for what I yearn for?
    Why this striving, why this eternal anxiety?
    Have I lost someone or has someone deserted me?
    Or have they changed their faces?
    So many people around and I miserably alone!

    Exalted

    How ashamed do I feel to know
    My age, my vocation, yet such yearning.
    A man so old, so ordinary, so mean
    Yet aspiring for your priceless love!
    I cry, repent, I curse myself
    To have lost my reason, self-restraint.
    Every night I resolve to curb my passion
    To kill the life in me, the love in soul.//
    Yet when I look at your glorious face,
    Hear the melody of your sweet smiles,
    See the divine light pervade on your cheeks,
    And the mischievous look in your eyes.
    Oh, how proud do I feel, how exulted
    To have loved someone so divine, so exalted.
